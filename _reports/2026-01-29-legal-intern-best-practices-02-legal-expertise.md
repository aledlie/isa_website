Legal professionals bring unique capabilities to AI companies across multiple domains.

## 2.1 Where Legal Skills Add Value

**AI Ethics and Responsible AI**
- Developing ethical guidelines for AI use
- Creating internal policies on AI in legal processes
- Advising on AI ethics governance frameworks
- Supporting Constitutional AI evaluation (at companies like Anthropic)

**Compliance and Regulatory Affairs**
- Interpreting legal statutes and regulations relevant to AI
- Conducting risk assessments for AI systems
- Ensuring compliance with EU AI Act, GDPR, CCPA, and emerging regulations
- Developing compliance management systems

**Policy and Government Affairs**
- Working on AI governance and policy projects
- Engaging with regulators and shaping global AI norms
- Supporting government relations initiatives
- Contributing to industry standards development

**Contracts and Commercial**
- Drafting AI-related contracts and licensing agreements
- Reviewing vendor agreements for AI tools
- Negotiating data sharing arrangements
- Managing intellectual property considerations

## 2.2 Key Organizations Hiring Legal Interns

**Major Tech Companies**
- [Microsoft Legal Intern Program](https://careers.microsoft.com/v2/global/en/celainternship): 12-week program with placement in specific practice groups, providing hands-on experience on cutting-edge legal issues in technology
- Google, Meta, and other tech giants offer similar legal department internships

**AI-Focused Companies**
- [OpenAI Residency](https://openai.com/residency/): Six-month program with $18,300/month stipend, though primarily technical
- Anthropic: Policy & Alignment Research Support roles for candidates with philosophy, law, or policy backgrounds
- Shield AI: Government compliance team positions for AI-related regulatory review

**Research Institutes and Think Tanks**
- [Institute for AI Policy and Strategy (IAPS)](https://governance.ai/): 3-month AI Policy Fellowship with $15,000 stipend
- [Future of Privacy Forum](https://fpf.org/us-policy-internships/): Legal, policy, and technical interns for AI, privacy, and emerging technology issues
- Center for Democracy and Technology: AI-governance fellowship for law school graduates
- [GovAI Summer Fellowship](https://www.governance.ai/post/summer-fellowship-2026-applied-track): Applied track for AI governance research

## 2.3 Typical Responsibilities

Legal interns at AI companies may:
- Research legal issues impacting business strategy
- Draft guidelines for ethical AI use and compliance
- Test and refine legal tech products
- Develop AI-driven compliance reporting tools
- Attend client meetings and product reviews
- Collaborate on cross-functional projects
